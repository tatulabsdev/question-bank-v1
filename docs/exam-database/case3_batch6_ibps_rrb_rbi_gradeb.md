# 1. IBPS RRB PO (Officer Scale I) — Graduate level, Regional Rural Banks

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conducting Body | IBPS (CRP-RRB) |
| Prelims | 80 questions, 80 marks, 45 minutes, **qualifying only**, sectional timing + sectional AND overall cutoffs. Only **2 sections**: Reasoning Ability, Quantitative Aptitude — **no English/GA at all at Prelims.** |
| Mains | 200 marks, 5 sections: Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness, English/Hindi Language, Computer Knowledge. Candidate chooses English or Hindi for the language section. Sectional timing applies. |
| Negative Marking | 0.25 marks per wrong answer, both stages |
| Interview | 100 marks, minimum 40% to qualify (35% for SC/ST/OBC/PwD) — Officer Scale I gets an interview; Office Assistant (Clerk, below) does not |
| Final Merit | Mains + Interview (Prelims never counts) |
| Key structural note | **IBPS RRB is the only banking-family exam researched so far with zero English at Prelims** — a genuinely distinct entry gate from IBPS PO/Clerk and SBI PO/Clerk, which all test English at Prelims. Multiple sources independently describe RRB PO as generally easier/more direct than SBI PO or IBPS PO, but note cutoffs run higher because of the volume of well-prepared candidates. |

# 2. IBPS RRB Clerk (Office Assistant, Multipurpose) — 12th-pass/Graduate level, Regional Rural Banks

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conducting Body | IBPS (CRP-RRB) |
| Prelims | 80 questions, 80 marks, 45 minutes, **qualifying only**. 2 sections: Reasoning Ability, Numerical Ability (40Q/40 marks each) — same no-English structure as RRB PO. **Source disagreement found**: one source describes Prelims as having no sectional timers at all ("jump freely between sections"), another states sectional timing was newly introduced in 2026 — flagging this honestly rather than picking one; confirm against the specific year's official notification before building timed-mock-test content. |
| Mains | 200 marks, 5 sections: Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness, English/Hindi Language, Computer Knowledge |
| Negative Marking | 0.25 marks per wrong answer, both stages |
| No Interview | Unlike RRB PO — final selection is 100% Mains marks |
| Language Accessibility | Confirmed as the most linguistically accessible banking exam in this database — candidates can attempt in **English, Hindi, or a regional language of their choice**, with full regional-language support (Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, etc.) newly confirmed for 2026. This is broader than IBPS/SBI Clerk's LPT-after-the-fact model — RRB Clerk lets candidates use their regional language from the start. |
| Key structural note | This is the exam most likely to be a genuine entry point for TryIT's non-English-dominant regional-language users specifically — no English section exists at Prelims at all, and the whole exam can be attempted in a regional language. If your translation-prioritization is aiming at "which exam most needs non-English/non-Hindi practice content," RRB Clerk is a strong candidate for that ranking, ahead of every other banking exam researched so far. |

# 3. RBI Grade B (Officer, General/DEPR/DSIM streams) — Graduate/Postgraduate level

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conducting Body | RBI Services Board |
| Streams | General (broadest), DEPR (Dept. of Economic & Policy Research — Master's in Economics standard), DSIM (Dept. of Statistics & Information Management) — each with a different Phase 1/2 syllabus; this batch covers General in detail |
| Phase 1 (General) | 200 questions, 200 marks, 120 minutes, **qualifying only**, sectional timing + sectional AND overall cutoffs. 4 sections: **General Awareness (80Q/80 marks — the single highest-weighted section of any banking-family exam researched so far)**, Reasoning Ability (60 marks), English Language (30 marks), Quantitative Aptitude (30 marks). |
| Phase 2 (General) | 300 marks, 3 papers: Economic & Social Issues (ESI — objective + descriptive), English (fully descriptive, typed on-screen), Finance & Management (FM — objective + descriptive). Sectional cutoffs apply on all 3 papers plus an overall cutoff. |
| Negative Marking | 0.25 marks (1-mark Qs) / 0.50 marks (2-mark Qs) per wrong answer in Phase 1 and the objective portions of Phase 2 (ESI, FM). **None on descriptive papers** (English, and the descriptive portions of ESI/FM). |
| Interview | 75 marks (20% of final selection weight) — includes a prior, unscored Psychometric Test whose results are shared with the interview panel |
| Final Merit | Phase 2 + Interview only (Phase 1 never counts) — 300 + 75 = 375 total |
| Key structural note | **RBI Grade B's Phase 1 gives General Awareness nearly 2.7x the weight of Reasoning and Quant combined isn't quite right — let's be precise: GA (80) alone exceeds Quant (30) + English (30) combined, and is higher than any single other section.** This is a sharp departure from every other banking exam in this database (IBPS/SBI PO/Clerk all keep GA roughly comparable to or lower than Reasoning/Quant at their respective stages) — RBI Grade B is structurally closer to UPSC's GK-heavy weighting than to standard banking-exam patterns, consistent with its reputation as "the toughest of all Bank exams" per multiple independent sources. |

## UPDATED CROSS-DATABASE FINDING

**Within "banking exams" as a category, RBI Grade B is structurally its own tier** — GA-dominant like UPSC rather than balanced like IBPS/SBI, plus a real descriptive-writing component (English Descriptive, ESI, FM) closer to UPSC Mains than to any Clerk/PO objective-only pattern. Treating "banking exam prep" as one undifferentiated content bucket would under-serve RBI Grade B aspirants specifically — they need GA/current-affairs depth and descriptive-writing practice that IBPS/SBI PO/Clerk aspirants don't.

**IBPS RRB (both PO and Clerk) forms its own accessible sub-tier within banking** — no English at Prelims, and Clerk specifically supports full regional-language attempts. This is your strongest banking-exam candidate for regional-language-first content.
