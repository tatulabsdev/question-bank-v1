# 1. NTSE (National Talent Search Examination) — Class 10 level

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conducting Body | NCERT (National Council of Educational Research and Training) at national level; State/UT bodies at Stage 1 |
| Structure | Two stages: Stage 1 (state-level, conducted by respective states/UTs) → Stage 2 (national, conducted by NCERT). Both stages have identical paper structure. |
| Paper 1 — MAT | Mental Ability Test: 100 MCQs, 100 marks, 120 minutes. Tests verbal and non-verbal reasoning (analogy, classification, series, blood relations, direction sense, coding-decoding, mirror image, paper folding, pattern recognition). |
| Paper 2 — SAT | Scholastic Aptitude Test: 100 MCQs, 100 marks, 120 minutes. Science (40Q) + Social Science (40Q) + Mathematics (20Q). Based on NCERT Class 9-10 syllabus. |
| Marking | +1 per correct answer. **No negative marking** in either stage. |
| Eligibility | Students currently studying in Class X from any recognized board. No minimum percentage at Stage 1; various state-specific criteria exist. |
| Volume | Historically largest school-level scholarship exam in India, though exact 2026 applicant numbers not confirmed — exam currently operating normally per sourced articles. |
| Key structural note | Science internal split (Physics/Chemistry/Biology within the 40Q) is not independently sourced — approximately equal thirds (~13-14Q each) based on past paper analysis, not official NCERT documentation. Social Science split (History/Geography/Civics/Economics) similarly estimated at equal quarters. |

# 2. NMMS (National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship) — Class 8 level

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conducting Body | State SCERTs (State Councils of Educational Research and Training) under MHRD/Ministry of Education |
| Structure | Single stage: MAT (90Q, 90 marks, 90 min) + SAT (90Q, 90 marks, 90 min). Total: 180 marks. |
| MAT | 90 MCQs testing verbal and non-verbal metacognitive abilities — reasoning, critical thinking, analogies, series formation, classification. Class 7-8 difficulty level. |
| SAT | 90 MCQs covering Science (~35Q), Social Studies (~35Q), Mathematics (~20Q) as taught in Classes 7 and 8 per NCERT/State Board syllabus. Note: exact split varies slightly by state — Telangana: Science 35Q, Social 35Q, Maths 20Q sourced; NCERT model shows similar split. |
| Marking | +1 per correct answer. **No negative marking**. Minimum qualifying: 40% aggregate MAT+SAT (32% for SC/ST/PH). |
| Eligibility | Class 8 students; parents' annual income ≤ ₹3.5 lakh; studying in government/government-aided schools. |
| Frequency | Exam dates vary by state; typically November-April across states. |
| Key structural note | Unlike NTSE (NCERT-run, uniform), NMMS is state-conducted, so exact question distribution can vary state to state. The 90/90/20 Science/Social/Maths split is the best available estimate from multiple state sources but should be treated as approximate, not as a single confirmed national figure. |

# 3. INSPIRE-SHE (Scholarship for Higher Education) — formerly KVPY

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conducting Body | DST (Department of Science & Technology), Government of India |
| KVPY Status | **Officially discontinued as a standalone aptitude test in 2022.** The KVPY fellowship was merged with the INSPIRE scheme. No KVPY aptitude test has been conducted since. |
| What replaced it | INSPIRE-SHE scholarship scheme. Students qualify through THREE channels: (1) Top 1% in Class 12 board exams pursuing natural/basic sciences at graduation. (2) Top 10,000 ranks in JEE Advanced. (3) Top rankers in NEET-UG pursuing basic sciences. |
| Amount | ₹80,000/year for 5 years during BSc/BS/integrated MSc. Additional ₹20,000 for summer research projects. |
| No separate exam | There is NO separate INSPIRE-SHE aptitude test to prepare for. Students who excel in Class 12 boards and/or JEE Advanced/NEET automatically qualify. |
| Key structural note for TryIT | Adding to registry as awareness entry only. No exam_syllabus_map rows appropriate since there is no standalone exam. Questions from physics, chemistry, biology, maths subjects already serve the overlapping JEE/NEET preparation that naturally feeds INSPIRE-SHE eligibility. |

## KEY OBSERVATIONS FOR QUESTION-BANK WEIGHTING

1. **Zero new topics needed.** NTSE and NMMS test exact subjects already in the 164-topic bank: reasoning (verbal + nonverbal), maths arithmetic/algebra, physics, chemistry, biology, GK history/geography/polity. This was a registry and tagging gap, not a content gap.

2. **No negative marking for either exam** — unlike most competitive govt exams. Questions tagged NTSE/NMMS should NOT be weighted toward negative-marking strategy tips in cross_exam_intelligence.

3. **NMMS difficulty level is L2-L3** (Class 7-8), **NTSE is L3-L4** (Class 9-10). Both are well below the floor of most competitive exam content. This means the existing lower-level questions (L2-L4) in the bank have immediate real exam relevance that was previously unrecognized because these exams weren't in the registry.

4. **Huge potential audience.** NTSE historically involved millions of applicants across all states. NMMS similarly large. Both underserved by existing edtech since most platforms focus on 11-12 and above. This age group (Class 8-10) is TryIT's strongest reach opportunity via Foundation Course.
